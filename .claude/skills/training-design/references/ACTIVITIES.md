# Activity Catalog — Training from the Back of the Room

Reference for selecting activities when designing training modules. Organized by the 4-Cs phase. Load this file when recommending activities for a specific module or format.

---

## Pre-Training: Priming

Activities to prepare learners before the session begins — reduces cognitive load and creates anticipation.

- **Blackout Bingo:** Send participants a bingo card with key terms before the training. They listen for the terms during the session and mark them off. Creates attentiveness and activates prior knowledge without effort.
- **Pre-Reading / Pre-Work:** Short article, video, or reflection question sent 1–2 days before. Max. 15 min. Establishes a common starting point.
- **One-Minute Opener:** At the very start of the session, give participants 60 seconds to write down everything they already know about the topic. No discussion yet — just activates memory.

## C1 — Connections (Opening, 5–10 min)

Goal: activate prior knowledge, connect participants to the topic and to each other.

### In-Person
- **Standing Survey:** Participants position themselves on a line across the room based on experience or opinion. Example: "Left = never used AI tools, Right = use them daily." No talking needed — positions create conversation naturally.
- **Fast Pass:** Each participant gets an index card with a question. They find a partner, discuss for 2 min, then swap cards and find a new partner. Energizes quickly.
- **Graffiti Wall:** Flipchart with an open prompt ("What comes to mind when you hear X?"). Participants write keywords as they enter or during a designated 3-min window.
- **Think-Pair-Share:** Think alone (1 min) → discuss with a neighbor (2 min) → optional plenary share.
- **Myth or Fact:** Statements on cards or projected. Participants sort them into True / False / Unsure individually or in pairs before any input.
- **Drop Ego Here:** A sign or sticky note exercise where participants write one assumption or bias they're bringing in and consciously set it aside. Sets tone for openness.

### Remote
- **Standing Survey** → Poll tool (Mentimeter, Miro, Slido). Show results live.
- **Fast Pass** → Breakout rooms, 2 min, random pairs. Give a question in the room.
- **Graffiti Wall** → Shared Miro/FigJam board, participants type or sticky-note.
- **Think-Pair-Share** → Individual reflection in chat (hidden), then breakout for pairs.
- **Myth or Fact** → Poll or drag-and-drop on Miro board.

## C2 — Concepts (Input, 15–20 min)

Goal: deliver new content in short blocks (≤10–15 min) with built-in interaction.

### Input Rules
- **10-Minute Rule:** Never more than 10–15 min of continuous trainer input. After every block, insert a participant activity before continuing.
- **Chunking:** Break content into 3–5 key ideas. One idea = one block.
- **Cognitive Load:** Avoid dense slides. Max. 3–5 bullets. Prefer visuals.

### Interaction Activities
- **Rapid Writing:** After an input block: "Write for 60 seconds what you just took away." No sharing required — the writing itself embeds the learning.
- **Teach-Back (Safe):** Participants explain a concept to a partner in their own words. Important: give a sentence starter ("One thing I understood is...") and always allow "Right to Pass" — no one is called on cold.
- **Jigsaw:** Divide content into sub-topics. Each small group studies one sub-topic, then groups mix and teach each other. Works well for complex content with parallel tracks.
- **Graphic Organizer:** Pre-structured worksheet participants fill in while listening — diagrams, tables, concept maps. Keeps attention focused and creates a personal reference document.
- **Signal Cards / Thumbs:** Green = understood, Red/Yellow = question or confusion. Trainer scans the room or screen continuously.
- **3 Before Me:** Before asking the trainer, participants must ask 3 peers first. Builds peer-to-peer knowledge transfer and reduces trainer-dependency.

### Remote Additions
- **Annotate Slides:** Participants annotate a shared slide with their answers or questions in real time.
- **Chat Waterfall:** Everyone writes their answer in chat but nobody sends until the trainer says "go." All answers appear simultaneously — avoids anchoring bias.

## C3 — Concrete Practice (Exercise, 15–30 min)

Goal: participants apply what they just learned — hands-on, with real materials or tools.

### Activity Types
- **Skill Practice:** Real task using actual tools or materials from the participant's context. Example: write a prompt, analyze a process, draft a plan. Most valuable activity type — always aim for this.
- **Case Study / Scenario:** Participants work through a realistic situation related to the topic. Better than abstract exercises.
- **Pair Work:** Two participants solve a task together. One drives, one observes and gives feedback. Then switch.
- **Teach-Back Advanced:** Small group prepares a 3-min explanation of a concept for another group. Forces consolidation.
- **Gallery Walk:** Results (on paper, cards, or whiteboards) are posted around the room. All participants circulate with sticky notes to comment, add, or question. Debrief follows.
- **Action Plan Draft:** Participants begin drafting their personal application plan during the training. Makes transfer concrete and immediate.
- **Peer Review:** Participants exchange outputs and give structured feedback (1 strength + 1 suggestion).

### Remote Adaptations
- **Pair Work** → Breakout rooms with screen sharing.
- **Gallery Walk** → Miro/FigJam board with timer; each group deposits results, all review and comment asynchronously before debrief.
- **Skill Practice** → Shared document or real tool open in screen share.
- **Body Break (Remote):** Between exercises, insert a 2-min physical break — stand up, stretch, walk to the kitchen. Essential for remote sessions over 90 min. Announce it explicitly.

### Body Breaks (In-Person & Remote)
For sessions over 90 min, insert a Body Break every 45–60 min:
- Stand up and stretch (1–2 min)
- Brief walk or position change
- Short breathing exercise
Especially critical for remote: attention drops sharply after 60–90 min without breaks.

## C4 — Conclusions (Closing, 5–10 min)

Goal: participants consolidate learning, assess their own progress, and commit to next steps.

### Activities
- **Each Teach:** Each participant states in one sentence their most important insight. Quick round, no discussion. Trainer does not add — let participants have the last word.
- **3-2-1:** Write: 3 things learned, 2 things to apply, 1 open question. Can be shared or kept private.
- **Action Plan:** "What will I do differently in the next 7 days?" — written individually, then optionally shared with a partner for accountability.
- **Gallery Walk (Results):** Groups post their Concrete Practice outputs. Brief appreciation round — acknowledge the work before the session ends.
- **One-Minute Closer:** Participants write on an index card: "The most important thing I'm taking away is ___." Cards can be collected as Level 2 measurement data.
- **Celebration:** Genuine, brief acknowledgment of the group's learning. Not performative — name something specific the group did well.

### For Series / Multi-Day Formats
- **Commitment Card:** Participants write one specific commitment and give it to a partner, who follows up at the next session.
- **Learning Log:** Participants maintain a running document across sessions. Closing = add today's key insight.

## Resistant Learners

Strategies when participants are disengaged or skeptical:

- **Lower the stakes:** Start with low-risk activities (writing, not speaking). Build up.
- **Right to Pass:** Always acknowledge this explicitly at the start. Never cold-call.
- **Connect to their context:** Ask "When have you seen this before?" rather than presenting abstract content.
- **Find the voluntary leader:** Identify one enthusiast early and give them a micro-role (e.g., timekeeper, scribe).
- **Acknowledge resistance openly:** "I know some of you may be skeptical about X — that's a fair reaction. Let's look at the evidence together."

## Remote-Specific Considerations

- **Camera fatigue:** Alternate between camera-on (interaction) and camera-off (individual work) activities.
- **Chat as a participation channel:** For participants uncomfortable speaking, chat is a valid first step. Read chat contributions aloud and acknowledge them.
- **Technical check at start:** Always build in 5 min for setup, even for experienced groups.
- **Breakout room logistics:** Assign rooms in advance. Give a specific task and a timer. Check in on rooms after 3 min.
- **Energy management:** Remote sessions should not exceed 3–4 hours without a substantial break (≥30 min). For full-day formats, split into AM/PM blocks with a real lunch break.
