1. Framework Overview & Purpose

The AI Transformation Framework – powered by CRAFT is an iterative approach designed to integrate AI into the entire Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) [1]. Its purpose is to deliver a holistic, iterative strategy that equally addresses technology, people, and processes while providing concrete tools, roles, and metrics for execution [1]. The framework targets internal project teams and is simultaneously positioned as an external consulting service [1].

2. The 5 CRAFT Dimensions

The framework operates across five concurrent dimensions that define what is steered, measured, and developed [1]:

  • C – Compliance & Governance: Regulatory guardrails, policies, works council integration, and EU AI Act alignment [1].
  • R – Readiness (Human & Cultural): Role development, skill building, psychological safety, and champion networks [1].
  • A – Adoption & Usage: Real-world daily AI usage, spread across SDLC phases, and shadow AI channeling [1].
  • F – Flow & Process Integration: SDLC analysis, bottleneck identification, AI intervention matching, and workflow embedding [1].
  • T – Technical Enablement: Tool provision, LLM deployment, IDE integration, and infrastructure setup [1].

3. Discover & Iterate Operating Model

The transformation runs on a structured 4-week iteration cycle where all CRAFT dimensions remain simultaneously active [1]:

  • Week 1: Orientation & Planning (Pulse Check, review, goal definition) [1].
  • Weeks 2–3: Implementation (tool rollout, workshops, skill building) [1].
  • Week 4: Review & Adjustment (retrospective, metrics, Readiness Gate) [1].

Readiness Gates determine the next path: Expand (adoption >60%, stable DORA), Deepen (positive trends), or Consolidate (governance changes or cultural resistance) [1].

4. KPI Framework & Dual Objectives

The framework enforces a dual-goal structure to prevent actionism or self-purpose [1]:

  • Lens 1 (Business Impact): Tracks concrete business problems and 2–3 derived metrics per cycle [1].
  • Lens 2 (AI Enablement): Tracks CRAFT scores across the 5 dimensions [1].

Measurement relies on a 3-layer KPI architecture: Delivery Outcomes (DORA metrics), AI Maturity (CRAFT model), and Business Impact (EBIT, time-to-market) [1]. Scoring uses a Pulse Check (median) and Deep-Dive (min-weighted formula to prevent hiding critical weaknesses) [1].

5. Tools & Deliverables

Operational execution is supported by concrete artifacts [1]:

  • CRAFT Explorer App: Web-based guided exploration tool (Vue 3 + Vite + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS + Chart.js) generating scores and radar charts [1].
  • AI Intervention Catalog: Maps SDLC phases to AI solutions with security-level alternatives, effort estimates, and expected impact [1].
  • Technical Playbooks: Setup guides covering IDE plugins, MCP server integration, prompt libraries, and air-gapped hardware sizing [1].
  • Additional Assets: Governance Starter Kit, Champion Onboarding Kit, Psychological Safety Workshop Design, and KPI Dashboard Templates [1].

7. Context Profiles, References & Next Steps

Interpretation is contextualized using four organizational profiles: Agile & Open, Enterprise Regulated, Public Sector, and Conglomerate Liberal [1]. The framework selectively adopts validated reference models (MITRE, Kotter, DORA, Lean VSM, SAFe, EY.ai, OWASP, Prosci, SEI) while discarding over-complexity or sales-oriented structures [1].

Open decisions include framework validation via pilot deployment, finalizing a hybrid license model (open framework + proprietary services), and launching an internal Community of Practice before external expansion [1].
