Every transformation starts with the sponsor question. Identify the concrete problem, define 2–3 individual Business Impact Metrics — and review them every 4-week cycle.

Lever

Our Insights

New AI technology alone
does not make a successful transformation

Our insight from studies and practice — three guiding principles.

⇄

Value Stream / Workflow First

We start at the bottlenecks in the value stream — not at the tools. AI interventions are placed where they have the greatest leverage on the Business Impact Metrics. Workflow redesign is the strongest EBIT lever (McKinsey 2025).

♡

Human-Centric Change

Technology without a change process produces shelfware. Role development, psychological safety and champions are not add-ons — they are prerequisites for adoption. We use ADKAR as the proven change model: Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement.

⟳

Iteratively Guided with Metrics

No linear phases. 4-week cycles with Readiness Gates. All 5 CRAFT dimensions active simultaneously — with shifting focus. Business Impact Check keeps the focus on the real problem.

These three insights are not wishful thinking — CRAFT operationalizes them: 5 measurable dimensions, clear roles, 4-week cycles.

Framework Architecture

The 5 CRAFT Dimensions

Operationalizing the three principles — simultaneously active, never complete, each with clear ownership.

C

Compliance & Governance

What is the team allowed to do? What must it do? — AI Policies, Works Council, EU AI Act, 3 security levels. An enabler, not an independent value driver.

R

Readiness — Human & Cultural

Can and do people want to use AI? — Skills, Psychological Safety, Role Development, Champions. Prerequisite for adoption.

A

Adoption & Usage

Is AI actually being used — broadly across the SDLC? — DAU, usage depth, Shadow AI → Managed AI. Primary value driver.

F

Flow & Process Integration

Where are the real bottlenecks — and where does AI have the greatest leverage? — Value Stream Mapping, AI Intervention Catalogue. Primary value driver.

T

Technical Enablement

What specific setup does the team need? — IDE integration, LLM deployment, Prompt Libraries, MCP Server. An enabler, not an independent value driver.

Measurement System

Measurement as a Driver of Change

Data drives decisions — not opinions. As little measurement as possible, as much as necessary. Automated wherever feasible.

Individual Business Impact Metrics + DORA (Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, Change Failure Rate, MTTR) + Value Stream (Cycle Time, Flow Efficiency). Automatically from CI/CD and ticketing system. This is where you see whether the transformation is solving the right problem.

5 dimensions × 5 maturity levels via CRAFT Explorer Pulse Check (15 min., every 4-week cycle). Spider diagram + diagnostics. Measures how effectively we use AI — the lever through which business value is created.

Most Common Trap in Scaling

A successful pilot is immediately rolled out to 20 teams. Without gradual expansion with Readiness Gates — and without checking whether Business Impact Metrics have followed — scaling regularly fails.

From Practice

Insights & Variations
from Practical Use

CRAFT is not a recipe, but a framework. These variations we have seen repeatedly — and they should be chosen consciously.

⇅

Depth + Breadth in Parallel

Pilot teams go deep into the workflow — while all others simultaneously receive curated AI Basics Nuggets from validated pilot learnings. Without breadth, the organization waits 4–6 months for any movement. → Dedicated slide ahead.

👥

Scaling by Size

In small setups (< 30 engineers), 1–2 people cover all focus roles. In large organizations, specialists are dedicated. AI Transformation Lead always remains 100 % — not splittable.

⚒

Context Profiles Instead of One-Size

Agile, Enterprise Regulated, Public Sector, Corporate Liberal: same questions, profile-specific weighting of answers. What is mandatory for a startup is a risk for a public authority — and vice versa.

⚠

Most important lesson from practice: Those who only go deep (pilot alone) lose the rest of the organization. Those who only go broad (tool rollout for all) produce shelfware. Depth and breadth — both are prerequisites.

Dual-Track Model

Depth and Breadth —
why two tracks are needed

Without depth: no sustainable change at workflow level. Without breadth: no visibility — and growing management impatience. Both are legitimate. Both need an answer.

⬤  Depth Track — 2 Pilot Teams

Full CRAFT process, 4-week cycles. Bottleneck analysis → AI intervention → measurable DORA delta.

Why depth is indispensable:
→ Bolting AI onto existing processes actively degrades delivery — −7 % stability with 25 % more AI usage without process adaptation (DORA 2024)
→ Measurable DORA improvements only occur at the workflow level — bottleneck first, then tool
→ Workflow redesign = strongest EBIT lever among 25 tested attributes (McKinsey 2025)
⬤  Breadth Track — all remaining teams

AI Basics Nuggets from validated pilot learnings. 1–2 h per team/month. Starts from Phase 2 in parallel.

Why breadth is indispensable:
→ Without the breadth track, the majority of teams wait 4–6 months for any contact with the transformation
→ Management impatience is real — and needs visible movement now, not in Cycle 4
→ Only governance-checked, validated interventions enter the breadth track — no uncontrolled tool rollout
⚠
ATTENTION: Even in the breadth track, tool rollout alone is not enough — literally nothing happens.
Teams without Awareness and Desire respond to new tools with indifference or pressure rather than curiosity. Technical Nuggets that meet ignorance or fear fizzle out. The solution: ADKAR first — even in the breadth track.
